02
Methodological Analysis

NDVI Analysis via LandSat-8 & Google Earth Engine

Vegetation health assessment using Normalized Difference Vegetation Index (NDVI) derived from LandSat-8 multispectral imagery, processed within Google Earth Engine's cloud-based geospatial platform.

Methodology

LandSat-8 OLI imagery was filtered by date range, cloud cover, and geographic bounds using the ee.ImageCollection API. NDVI was computed as (NIR − Red) / (NIR + Red) using Bands 5 and 4. A median composite was generated to reduce atmospheric noise, classified into vegetation density categories, and exported as a GeoTIFF for downstream analysis in ArcGIS.

Published Research

AVIRIS-Classic vs AVIRIS-NG: Comparative Analysis

A comparative evaluation of NASA's AVIRIS-Classic and AVIRIS-NG imaging spectrometers, assessing spectral range, spatial resolution, and data quality across matched acquisition zones in California. Quantifies generational improvements and identifies scenarios where each instrument holds a performance advantage.

Primary analysis in R for statistical comparison of sensor outputs; spatial data managed and visualized in ArcGIS. Co-authored the resulting research paper at Northeastern University.
